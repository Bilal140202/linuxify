# Code of Conduct — Linuxify

> This Code of Conduct governs all participation in the Linuxify community. It is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/), customized for Linuxify's specific channels, governance structure, and community norms. It applies everywhere Linuxify community members gather — Discord, GitHub, Reddit, Mastodon, conferences, and any other space where Linuxify is the topic.

The Linuxify community exists to make Android a first-class developer platform. That mission is hard. It requires sustained effort from people with different skills, backgrounds, and perspectives, working together over years. A Code of Conduct is not a formality — it is the social infrastructure that makes sustained collaboration possible. We take it seriously, we enforce it fairly, and we update it when we learn better.

---

## 1. Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community. This pledge is not a passive statement — it is a commitment to actively create the kind of community we want to be part of. We recognize that harassment and exclusion are often subtle, often unintentional, and often invisible to those not targeted by them. We commit to listening when we are told that our words or actions have caused harm, even — especially — when we did not intend that harm.

We also recognize that the Linuxify community spans many countries, cultures, and languages. What is friendly in one context may be cold in another; what is direct in one culture may be rude in another. We give each other grace for cultural differences, and we ask each other to adjust when our default style is causing friction. The goal is communication that lands well, not communication that is "correct" by any one cultural standard.

## 2. Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- **Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.** We are all volunteers (or, in rare cases, sponsored part-timers) doing difficult work on top of the rest of our lives. Treat each other accordingly.
- **Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.** Technical disagreements are normal and healthy. Personal attacks are not. The line is whether you are arguing with the idea or attacking the person.
- **Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.** Code review is the most common place this comes up. Reviewers: be specific, be kind, use [conventional comment prefixes](docs/16-community/contribution-guidelines.md#7-code-review-guidelines). Contributors: assume reviewers want to help; if a review feels harsh, ask for clarification before responding.
- **Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes.** We all make them. The measure of a community is not whether mistakes happen, but how they are handled when they do.
- **Learning from the experience and growing as community members.** A Code of Conduct violation is not a permanent stain — it is an opportunity to learn, apologize, and do better.
- **Focusing on what is best for the overall community, not just ourselves.** Decisions should be made with the community's long-term health in mind, not short-term individual preference.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.** This is a developer community, not a dating platform. There is no gray area here.
- **Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.** "Trolling" includes deliberately provoking emotional responses, feigning ignorance to waste people's time, and sealioning (persistently demanding evidence for an established position).
- **Public or private harassment.** Repeatedly messaging someone who has asked you to stop; doxing (publishing private information without consent); sustained off-topic criticism in channels where the person cannot avoid it.
- **Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission.** This includes information that is technically public but not widely known — e.g., finding someone's home address from a domain registration and posting it in Discord.
- **Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.** This is the catch-all. If you are unsure whether something is appropriate, it probably is not. Ask yourself: "would I say this in front of my coworkers, my family, and a journalist?" If the answer is no, do not say it here.

The maintainers are responsible for clarifying and applying these standards. They may, at their discretion, remove or reject contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and they will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate. The standards apply in all community spaces — see [§4 Scope](#4-scope).

## 3. Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders (the [maintainer team](docs/16-community/contribution-guidelines.md#14-governance)) are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct. They will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

Community leaders have the additional responsibility of modeling the behavior they expect from the community. A maintainer who violates the Code of Conduct does more damage than a non-maintainer who does the same, because their behavior signals what is acceptable at the highest level of the project. Maintainers who violate the Code of Conduct are held to a stricter standard, not a looser one.

Enforcement actions are documented privately (in the maintainer team's private channel) and, when the affected parties consent, summarized publicly. We aim for transparency about *what* enforcement actions were taken, while protecting the privacy of the people involved. The [annual contributor report](docs/15-roadmap/release-roadmap.md#14-funding--sustainability) includes aggregate statistics on Code of Conduct reports and outcomes (e.g., "3 reports filed, 2 warnings issued, 1 temporary ban, 0 permanent bans").

## 4. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:

- **Discord server** (`discord.gg/linuxify`) — all channels, voice and text, including DMs between community members about Linuxify.
- **GitHub repositories** — `linuxify/linuxify`, `linuxify/linuxify-docs`, `linuxify/linuxify-patches`, `linuxify/registry`, `linuxify/telemetry-server`, and any future repos. This includes issues, PRs, commit messages, code comments, release notes, and GitHub Discussions.
- **Reddit** — `/r/linuxify` and any thread where Linuxify is the topic, when a participant's behavior in that thread affects the Linuxify community.
- **Mastodon** — the `@linuxify@hachyderm.io` account and replies to Linuxify posts, when behavior rises to the level of harassment.
- **Conferences and meetups** — Linuxify-sponsored events, talks about Linuxify at any event, and informal gatherings of Linuxify community members at events.
- **Social media** — when a Linuxify community member's behavior on their personal social media rises to the level of harassment of another Linuxify community member, and the conduct is brought to the maintainer team's attention.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing the community include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event. If you are unsure whether you are "officially representing" the community, you probably are not — but ask a maintainer if you want clarity.

The Code of Conduct does **not** apply to purely private disputes between community members that have no connection to the project. If two community members have a personal conflict unrelated to Linuxify, that is between them; the maintainer team is not a court of general jurisdiction. However, if the conflict spills into Linuxify spaces (e.g., one person follows the other into Linuxify Discord to continue the dispute), the conduct in Linuxify spaces is within scope.

## 5. Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at **`conduct@linuxify.sh`**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

The `conduct@linuxify.sh` email is monitored by the [lead maintainer](docs/16-community/contribution-guidelines.md#14-governance) and one other maintainer designated as the CoC response team. To avoid conflicts of interest, the CoC response team rotates quarterly and excludes any maintainer who is the subject of a current report.

**Confidentiality.** All reports are treated with strict confidentiality. The identity of the reporter is shared only with the members of the CoC response team and, on a need-to-know basis, with other maintainers involved in the enforcement decision. The reporter's identity is never shared with the reported person without the reporter's explicit consent.

**Response time.** The CoC response team acknowledges receipt of every report within **48 hours**. The acknowledgment includes: who received the report, what the next steps are, and an estimated timeline for a substantive response. Substantive responses typically take 1–2 weeks, depending on the complexity of the report and the need to gather additional information.

**Outcomes.** Possible outcomes of a Code of Conduct investigation include:

- **No action** — the reported behavior does not constitute a violation, or the report cannot be substantiated.
- **Correction** — a private message to the reported person explaining why their behavior was inappropriate and what is expected going forward.
- **Warning** — a formal warning recorded in the maintainer team's private log. A second warning may result in a temporary ban.
- **Temporary ban** — the reported person is removed from Linuxify community spaces for a defined period (typically 1–6 months). The banned person is told the duration, the reason, and the conditions for return.
- **Permanent ban** — the reported person is permanently removed from all Linuxify community spaces. This is reserved for severe or repeated violations.

The reporter is informed of the outcome (but not the specific details of any private conversations with the reported person). The reported person is informed of the outcome and the rationale. Both parties have the right to ask for reconsideration once, with new information; the lead maintainer's decision on reconsideration is final.

## 6. Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct. The guidelines are designed to be fair, consistent, and proportional. They are not a rigid formula — severity can skip levels when the behavior warrants it.

### 6.1 Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested. No further action is taken if the behavior does not recur.

**Example**: A community member makes a joke in Discord that relies on a stereotype about a nationality. Another member reports it. The CoC response team sends a private message explaining why the joke was inappropriate, asking the member to avoid similar jokes in the future, and noting that no further action is being taken. The member apologizes and does not repeat the behavior.

### 6.2 Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions that, while not severe enough to warrant a ban, demonstrates a pattern of behavior inconsistent with community standards.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of 1–7 days. The warning is recorded in the maintainer team's private log. Continuing the behavior during the warning period, or after it ends, may result in a temporary or permanent ban.

**Example**: A community member repeatedly leaves harsh, dismissive code review comments (despite a prior correction). The CoC response team issues a warning, asks the member to take a 3-day break from reviewing PRs, and notes that further similar behavior will result in a temporary ban.

### 6.3 Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of 1–6 months. The banned person may not post in Discord, GitHub, Reddit, or Mastodon (in Linuxify-related contexts); may not open PRs or issues; and may not attend Linuxify community calls. The ban is enforced by the maintainer team via platform-specific moderation tools (Discord bans, GitHub blocks, etc.). The banned person is welcome back at the end of the ban period, provided they have not violated the ban itself.

**Example**: A community member sends a series of insulting direct messages to a contributor after a technical disagreement. The contributor reports it. The CoC response team issues a 30-day ban from all Linuxify community spaces, with a clear statement of what behavior led to the ban and what is expected on return.

### 6.4 Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community. The person is removed from all Linuxify community spaces and is not welcome back. Their existing contributions remain in the project (subject to the MIT license), but they may not contribute further.

**Example**: A community member is found to be doxing other contributors — publishing their real names, employers, and home cities in Discord and on a personal blog. The CoC response team issues a permanent ban, removes the offending messages, and notifies the affected contributors. Local law enforcement is contacted if the doxing includes threats.

### 6.5 Severity Escalation

The CoC response team may, at its discretion, skip levels when the severity of the violation warrants it. A single instance of doxing, threats of violence, or sexual harassment may result in an immediate temporary or permanent ban without prior correction or warning. The team documents the rationale for any escalation in the private log.

Conversely, the team may reduce a recommended consequence when mitigating factors are present — e.g., a genuine apology, a first offense, a cultural misunderstanding, or evidence that the behavior was out of character due to a personal crisis. Reductions are documented in the private log with the rationale.
